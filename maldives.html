MALDIVES

ABOUT:

The Maldives is the smallest Asia. It is the capital and the most populated city, traditionally
called the "King's Island", where the ancient royal dynasties ruled from its central location.
The Maldives has been inhabited for over 2,500 years. Documented contact with the outside world
began around 947 AD when Arab travelers began visiting the islands. In the 12th century, partly
due to the importance of the Arabs and Persians as traders in the Indian Ocean, Islam reached
the Maldivian Archipelago.The Maldives was soon consolidated as a sultanate, developing strong
commercial and cultural ties with Asia and Africa. From the mid-16th century, the region came
under the increasing influence of European colonial powers, with the Maldives becoming a British
protectorate in 1887. Independence from the United Kingdom came in 1965, and a presidential
republic was established in 1968 with an elected People's Majlis. The ensuing decades have seen
political instability, efforts at democratic reform, and environmental challenges posed by climate
change and rising sea levels.[13] The Maldives became a founding member of the South Asian Association
for Regional Cooperation (SAARC).

FOOD:

Maldives is known for its seafood, coconut-based dishes, and flavorful curries. Popular Maldivian foods include
Garudhiya (fish soup), Mas Huni (tuna and coconut), Masroshi (stuffed flatbread), and Saagu Bondibai (sago pudding).
Spices like chili, curry leaves, cumin, and cardamom are frequently used to enhance the flavors.

Garudhiya:

: A clear fish broth, often made with tuna, and a staple in Maldivian cuisine.

Mas Huni:

A breakfast dish with shredded smoked tuna, coconut, onion, and chili, typically eaten with Roshi (flatbread).

Masroshi:

A fried, stuffed flatbread filled with tuna, coconut, and spices.

Saagu Bondibai:

A sweet pudding made with sago, condensed milk, and flavored with cardamom and rose essence.

Kulhi Boakibaa:

A savory fish cake made with rice, onions, coconut, and smoked tuna.

Bis Keemiya:

A savory pastry or turnover filled with tuna and hard-boiled eggs.

Rihaakuru:

A thick, dark brown fish paste or sauce made from fish broth.

Gulha:

Small, savory, deep-fried balls of dough filled with a mixture of tuna, coconut, and spices.

Aluvi Boakibaa:

A sweet cassava and coconut cake.

Boshi Mashuni:

A salad made with shredded banana flowers, coconut, and spices.

Hedhikaa:

A general term for Maldivian savory snacks, often served with afternoon tea.

Dhon Riha:

A creamy and spicy tuna curry with coconut, mango, cinnamon, and ginger.

Banbukeyo Harisa:

A breadfruit curry cooked with tuna, coconut, and spices.

WHAT MALIDIVES IS FAMOUS FOR:

The Maldives is famous for its stunning turquoise waters, pristine white-sand beaches, vibrant coral reefs,
and abundant marine life. It's also renowned for luxurious overwater bungalows, exciting water sports like
snorkeling and diving, and unique experiences such as bioluminescent beaches and dolphin and whale watching.

Natural Beauty:

Turquoise Waters and Beaches:

The Maldives boasts crystal-clear, shallow lagoons with shades of blue that are remarkably clear, complemented
by powdery white sand beaches.

Vibrant Coral Reefs:

The islands are surrounded by thriving coral reefs, home to a diverse array of marine life, making it a
paradise for snorkeling and diving enthusiasts

Marine Life:

Snorkelers and divers can encounter whale sharks, manta rays, sea turtles, reef sharks, and various colorful fish species.

Bioluminescent Beaches:

Some beaches, like Vaadhoo Island, exhibit a mesmerizing bioluminescent phenomenon where the waters glow blue at night,
creating a "Sea of Stars" effect.

Sunsets and Sunrises:

The Maldives is known for its breathtaking sunsets and sunrises over the Indian Ocean.

Activities:

Snorkeling and Scuba Diving:

Explore the coral reefs and marine life through organized excursions or private charters.

Water Sports:

Enjoy jet skiing, sea bobbing, surfing, and other water activities in the warm Indian Ocean.

Dolphin and Whale Watching:

Join organized cruises to spot various dolphin and whale species.

Deep Sea Fishing:

Engage in fishing excursions to catch various fish species.

Island Hopping:

Explore different islands, each with unique characteristics and offerings.

Overwater Bungalows:

Stay in luxurious overwater bungalows, a signature feature of Maldives resorts.

Other Aspects:

Luxury Resorts:

The Maldives is a premier destination for high-end resorts with world-class amenities and services.

Dhivehi Cuisine:

Enjoy a unique blend of Sri Lankan and Indian flavors with a focus on seafood and coconut-based dishes.

Unique Experiences:

The Maldives offers a variety of unique experiences like swimming with sharks or experiencing bioluminescent beaches

Balinese Cuisine:

Aromatic spices, fresh ingredients, and unique cooking techniques characterize Balinese cuisine, with dishes
like Nasi Goreng and Sate Lilit.
