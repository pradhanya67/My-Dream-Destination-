KYOTO

ABOUT:

Kyoto is one of the oldest municipalities in Japan, having been chosen in 794 as the new seat of Japan's imperial court by Emperor Kanmu.
The original city, named Heian-kyō, was arranged in accordance with traditional Chinese feng shui following the model of the ancient
Chinese capitals of Chang'an and Luoyang. The emperors of Japan ruled from Kyoto in the following eleven centuries until 1869.
It was the scene of several key events of the Muromachi period, Sengoku period, and the Boshin War, such as the Ōnin War, the Honnō-ji Incident,
the Kinmon incident, and the Battle of Toba–Fushimi. The capital was relocated from Kyoto to Tokyo after the Meiji Restoration.
The modern municipality of Kyoto was established in 1889. The city was spared from large-scale destruction during World War II and,
as a result, its prewar cultural heritage has mostly been preserved.

Kyoto is considered the cultural capital of Japan and is a major tourist destination. The agency for cultural affairs of the national
government is headquartered in the city. It is home to numerous Buddhist temples, Shinto shrines, palaces and gardens, some of which
have been designated collectively as a World Heritage Site by UNESCO. Prominent landmarks include the Kyoto Imperial Palace,
Kiyomizu-dera, Kinkaku-ji, Ginkaku-ji, and Kyoto Tower. The internationally renowned video game company Nintendo is based in Kyoto.
Kyoto is also a center of higher learning in the country, and its institutions include Kyoto University, the second-oldest
university in Japan.

FOOD:

Kyoto is famous for its refined and delicate cuisine, with specialties like Kaiseki, Shojin Ryori, and Obanzai. Other notable foods
include tofu, Yuba (tofu skin), Matcha desserts, and dishes like Saba Sushi (mackerel sushi) and Yudofu (hot tofu). Additionally,
Kyoto is known for its traditional sweets called Wagashi and local specialties like Yatsuhashi.

Kaiseki:

A traditional multi-course Japanese haute cuisine, known for its seasonal ingredients and artistic presentation.

Shojin Ryori:

Buddhist vegetarian cuisine, emphasizing mindfulness and the natural flavors of ingredients like tofu, yuba, and seasonal vegetables.

Obanzai:

Kyoto-style home cooking, featuring simple, seasonal dishes passed down through generations.

Tofu:

Celebrated for its delicate texture and rich flavor, often featured in dishes like Yudofu (boiled tofu) and Agedofu (deep-fried tofu).

Yuba:

Tofu skin, a Kyoto specialty with a unique texture, often enjoyed in various dishes.

Matcha Desserts:

Kyoto, being a center for Matcha production, offers a wide array of Matcha-flavored sweets and drinks.

Saba Sushi (Mackerel Sushi):

A local favorite, often enjoyed during festivals and celebrations.

Yudofu:

A simple dish of tofu boiled in a kelp broth, often served with a dipping sauce.

Wagashi:

Traditional Japanese sweets, meticulously crafted to reflect the seasons and specific themes.

Yatsuhashi:

A famous Kyoto sweet made from glutinous rice flour, sugar, and cinnamon.

Nishin Soba:

A local specialty featuring soba noodles topped with simmered herring.

Kyoto Ramen:

A variety of ramen styles, reflecting the city's diverse food culture.

WHAT KYOTO IS FAMOUS FOR:

Kyoto is renowned for its rich cultural heritage, including numerous temples, shrines, and historical sites, making it a major tourist
destination in Japan. It was the capital of Japan for over a thousand years and served as the residence of the Emperor. The city is
also known for its traditional crafts, refined cuisine, and vibrant festivals.

Capital City:

Kyoto served as the imperial capital of Japan for over a millennium, from 794 to 1868, shaping its cultural identity.

Temples and Shrines:

Kyoto boasts an impressive number of temples and shrines, with over 2,000, including UNESCO World Heritage Sites like Kiyomizu-dera,
Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion), and Fushimi Inari-taisha Shrine.

Imperial Palace:

The Kyoto Imperial Palace, the former residence of the Emperor, is a significant historical landmark

World Heritage Sites:

Kyoto has 17 UNESCO World Heritage sites, showcasing its historical and cultural importance.

Traditional Arts and Crafts:

Kyoto is known for traditional crafts like pottery, textiles, and Nishiki Market, a vibrant food market with over 400 years of history.

Festivals:

Gion Matsuri, Aoi Matsuri, and Jidai Matsuri are some of the famous festivals celebrated in Kyoto.

Geisha Culture:

Kyoto is known for its geisha districts, where traditional geishas (Japanese female entertainers) can still be seen.
