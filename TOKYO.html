TOKYO

ABOUT:

Before the 17th century, Tokyo, then known as Edo, was mainly a fishing village.
It gained political prominence in 1603 when it became the seat of the Tokugawa shogunate.
By the mid-18th century, Edo was among the world's largest cities, with over a million residents.
After the Meiji Restoration (1868), the imperial capital in Kyoto was moved to Edo, and the city
was renamed Tokyo (lit. 'Eastern Capital'). Tokyo was greatly damaged by the 1923 Great Kantō
earthquake and by allied bombing raids during World War II. From the late 1940s, Tokyo underwent
rapid reconstruction and expansion, which fueled the Japanese economic miracle, in which Japan's
economy became the second-largest in the world at the time, behind that of the United States.
As of 2023, Tokyo is home to 29 of the world's 500 largest companies, as listed in the annual
Fortune Global 500—the second highest number of any city.

Tokyo was the first city in Asia to host the Summer Olympics and Paralympics, in 1964 and then in 2021.
It also hosted three G7 summits, in 1979, 1986, and 1993. Tokyo is an international hub of research and
development and an academic center, with several major universities, including the University of Tokyo,
the top-ranking university in Japan. Tokyo Station is the central hub for the Shinkansen,
the country's high-speed railway network; and the city's Shinjuku Station is the world's busiest train station.
Tokyo Skytree is the world's tallest tower. The Tokyo Metro Ginza Line, which opened in 1927, is the oldest
underground metro line in the Asia–Pacific region.

FOOD:

Tokyo offers a wide array of famous foods, with sushi, ramen, and yakitori being some of the most iconic.
Other notable dishes include tempura, tonkatsu, soba noodles, and monjayaki. Tokyo is also known for
its unique local specialties like chanko nabe (a hot pot dish popular with sumo wrestlers) and
Fukagawa meshi (a rice dish with clams).

Sushi:

Nigiri-zushi, a type of sushi consisting of a slice of fish over a mound of vinegared rice,
is a popular and fast food that originated in Tokyo.

Ramen:

Tokyo is known for its classic soy sauce-based ramen broth, which is rich in umami.

Yakitori:

Grilled chicken skewers, often found at street food stalls and izakayas, are a beloved Tokyo street food.

Tempura:

Deep-fried seafood and vegetables, tempura, also has its roots in Tokyo's Edo period.

Tonkatsu:

Breaded and deep-fried pork cutlets, tonkatsu, offer a satisfying contrast of crispy and juicy.

Soba:

Buckwheat noodles, soba, are a popular and traditional dish in Tokyo.

Monjayaki:

A Tokyo specialty, monjayaki is a runny pancake-like dish cooked on a hot plate, often with cabbage and seafood.

Chanko Nabe:

This hearty hot pot dish is associated with sumo wrestlers and can be enjoyed in the Ryogoku area.

Fukagawa Meshi:

A rice dish topped with a miso-based stew of clams and leeks, Fukagawa meshi is a local specialty.

Tsukemen:

A type of ramen where noodles are eaten dipped in a separate bowl of broth.

Gyukatsu:

A breaded and deep-fried beef cutlet, a newer food trend in Tokyo.

WHAT TOKYO IS FAMOUS FOR :

Tokyo is famous for its unique blend of traditional culture and modern innovation, offering a wide array of
attractions and experiences. It's known for its iconic landmarks like Tokyo Skytree and Tokyo Tower,
vibrant shopping and entertainment districts like Shibuya and Harajuku, and delicious cuisine, including
sushi and tempura. Additionally, Tokyo is renowned for its efficient public transportation, diverse museums,
and beautiful parks and gardens like Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden.

Tokyo Skytree:

A broadcasting and observation tower, offering panoramic views of the city.

Tokyo Tower:

A symbol of Japan's post-war rebirth and a popular tourist spot.

Senso-ji Temple:

Tokyo's oldest temple, located in the historic Asakusa district.

Meiji Jingu Shrine:

A tranquil oasis dedicated to Emperor Meiji and Empress Shoken.

Shibuya Crossing:

The world's busiest pedestrian intersection, a must-see spectacle.

Imperial Palace:

The residence of Japan's Imperial Family, a place of historical and cultural significance.
