CAIRO

ABOUT:

The area that would become Cairo was part of ancient Egypt, as the Giza pyramid complex
and the ancient cities of Memphis and Heliopolis are near-by. Located near the Nile Delta,
the predecessor settlement was Fustat following the Muslim conquest of Egypt in 641 next to
an existing ancient Roman fortress, Babylon. Subsequently, Cairo was founded by the
Fatimid dynasty in 969. It later superseded Fustat as the main urban centre during the Ayyubid
and Mamluk periods (12thâ€“16th centuries).

Cairo has since become a longstanding centre of political and cultural life, and is titled
"the city of a thousand minarets" for its preponderance of Islamic architecture. Cairo's
historic center was awarded World Heritage Site status in 1979. Cairo is considered a
World City with a "Beta +" classification according to GaWC.

Cairo has the oldest and largest film and music industry in the Arab world, as well as
Egypt's oldest institution of higher learning, Al-Azhar University. Many international
media, businesses, and organizations have regional headquarters in the city;
the Arab League has had its headquarters in Cairo for most of its existence.

Cairo, like many other megacities, suffers from high levels of pollution and traffic.
The Cairo Metro, opened in 1987, is the oldest metro system in Africa,[11] and ranks
amongst the fifteen busiest in the world,[12] with over 1 billion[13] annual passenger
rides. The economy of Cairo was ranked first in the Middle East in 2005,[14] and
43rd globally on Foreign Policy's 2010 Global Cities Index.[15]

FAMOUS FOOD IN CIARO

Koshari is the most famous food to try in Cairo, particularly when visiting the Great Pyramid of Giza.
It's a popular, affordable, and filling dish considered by many to be Egypt's national dish.
Koshari is a flavorful mix of rice, lentils, pasta (macaroni or spaghetti), fried onions, and
various sauces (tomato, garlic, and spicy). It's widely available as street food and in restaurants,
making it a perfect choice for refueling after exploring the pyramids.

  • Mahshi:

    Rice-stuffed vegetables or grape leaves

  • Falafel (Taamiya):

    Deep-fried balls or patties made from ground chickpeas or fava beans

  • Shawarma:

    Thinly sliced meat (chicken or lamb) cooked on a rotating spit.

  • Fatteh:

    A traditional Egyptian dish with layers of bread, rice, and meat, often topped with a yogurt sauce.

  • Ful Medames:

    A stew of cooked fava beans, often served with olive oil, cumin, and other toppings.

  • Feteer Meshaltet:

    An Egyptian layered pastry, similar to pizza.

  • Hawawshi:

    A stuffed bread with minced meat and onions, a local favorite near the pyramids.

WHAT CIARO IS FAMOUS FOR :

Beyond the pyramids, Cairo is renowned for its historical sites, museums, and vibrant cultural experiences.
The Great Sphinx, the Egyptian Museum, and the Mosque of Muhammad Ali are major attractions. Additionally,
Cairo offers bustling markets like Khan el-Khalili, the Nile River, and a thriving nightlife scene.
SUCH AS:

  • The Great Sphinx:

    A limestone statue of a mythical creature with the body of a lion and the head of a human, located near the pyramids.

  • The Egyptian Museum:

    Home to an extensive collection of ancient Egyptian artifacts, including treasures from Tutankhamun's tomb.

  • Mosque of Muhammad Ali:

    A striking mosque located in the Citadel of Saladin, known for its Ottoman-style architecture.

  • Khan el-Khalili:

    A historic marketplace with narrow, winding streets, filled with shops selling souvenirs, spices, and more.

  • Nile River:

    A major waterway that runs through Cairo, offering opportunities for cruises and sightseeing.

  • Citadel of Saladin:

    A medieval Islamic fortress that offers panoramic views of the city and houses the Mosque of Muhammad Ali.

  • Al-Azhar Mosque:

    • One of the oldest and most prestigious universities in the Islamic world, located in Islamic Cairo.

  • Coptic Cairo:

    A historic area with ancient Coptic churches and the Coptic Museum, showcasing Coptic art and history.

  • Islamic Cairo:

    A UNESCO World Heritage site with numerous mosques, madrasas (religious schools), and other historical buildings.

  • Cairo Tower:

    A modern landmark offering panoramic views of the city.

  • Manial Palace and Museum:

    A historic palace showcasing Ottoman-era architecture and decorative arts

  • Museum of Islamic Art:

    A museum housing a vast collection of Islamic art and artifacts.

  • Qalawun Complex:

    A complex of buildings including a mosque, mausoleum, and hospital, showcasing Mamluk architecture.
